================================================================================
WAFER RECONSTRUCTION SPECIFICATION
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A structure is built by assembling a sequence of cylinder sections called
wafers.  Each wafer is cut from a cylinder by two oblique plane cuts that
form its entry and exit faces.  Wafers are constructed independently from
the cut list and then assembled by aligning the mating elliptical faces
using reference marks.

No global coordinate system is required.  The shape of the assembled
structure emerges entirely from the geometry of the individual wafers and
the mark-alignment assembly rule.


TERMINOLOGY
-----------
Cylinder axis   The axis of the cylinder from which a wafer is sawed.
                In each wafer's local frame, this is the Z direction,
                pointing from entry face (Z = 0) to exit face (Z = L).

Blade angle (θ) The tilt of the cutting plane from perpendicular to the
                cylinder axis, in degrees.  θ = 0° produces a flat circle;
                larger θ produces a more elongated ellipse.

Major-axis       The direction in the cylinder cross-section plane (XY)
direction (M)   oriented so that the +M endpoint of the major axis is the
                MARKED (outward-protruding) endpoint on each face.  On the
                entry face the +M endpoint protrudes toward the entry side
                (Z < 0); on the exit face the +M' endpoint protrudes toward
                the exit side (Z > L).  Thus M points AWAY from Z = 0 on
                the exit face, not toward it.  All measurements of Rot° are
                rotations about Z within this cross-section plane.

Mark            A physical reference point on the major axis of each
                elliptical face, used to establish assembly alignment.
                Defined precisely in the ELLIPSE GEOMETRY section.


CUT LIST STRUCTURE
------------------
Each row in the cut list defines a single cut (one saw pass through the
cylinder).  One cut simultaneously creates:

    - the EXIT face of wafer i
    - the ENTRY face of wafer i + 1

Each row contains these columns used for reconstruction:

    Length   The distance along the cylinder axis from the preceding cut
             to this cut.  This is the nominal length of the wafer whose
             exit face this cut defines (center-to-center along Z).
             Note: the physical cutting jig contacts the highest point of
             the preceding ellipse, not its center, so the jig reading
             differs from this center-to-center value by R·tan(θ_prev).
             The original build must account for this offset; for
             reconstruction purposes the center-to-center definition is
             correct.

    Blade°   The blade angle θ of this cut.  Blade° is always ≥ 0.  At
             S-curve inflection points where the tilt direction reverses,
             the reversal is encoded in Rot° as an offset of approximately
             180° rather than by a sign change in Blade°.  This is
             equivalent to what the Cylinder° "180° offset" convention
             handles during physical sawing.

    Rot°     The rotation of the exit major-axis direction relative to the
             entry major-axis direction within this wafer, measured in
             degrees around the cylinder axis (Z).

The Cylinder° column is a physical sawing guide and is NOT used for
reconstruction.

Open curve:  An implicit row 0 with Blade° = 0 exists, defining the entry
             face of the first wafer as a flat circle.

Closed curve: The cut list wraps around.  Row N defines the exit face of
              wafer N and also the entry face of wafer 1.


ELLIPSE GEOMETRY
----------------
A cut with blade angle θ through a cylinder of radius R produces an
ellipse with:

    Minor semi-axis:  R
                      in the direction perpendicular to M within the cut
                      plane (call this direction N_minor = Z × M)

    Major semi-axis:  R / cos(θ)
                      in the direction M within the cut plane

M is defined such that the +M endpoint of the major axis is the MARKED
endpoint on both faces:

    Entry face:
        The +M endpoint of the major axis lies at Z < 0, on the side
        closer to the beginning (entry end) of the cylinder.
        MARK this endpoint.

    Exit face:
        The +M (or +M') endpoint of the major axis lies at Z > L, on the
        side farther from the beginning of the cylinder.
        MARK this endpoint.

The entry and exit faces tilt AWAY from each other: the marked (+M)
endpoint of the entry face extends toward −Z while the marked (+M')
endpoint of the exit face extends toward +Z.  The wafer is thicker on the
marked side and thinner on the opposite side.

Concretely, in the local (M, Z) cross-section plane:

    Entry face at Z = 0:   z(x) = −x · tan(θ_entry)
    Exit face  at Z = L:   z(x) = L + x · tan(θ_exit)

where x is the coordinate along M (or M').  The "negative blade angle"
description for the exit face means the exit tilt is in the opposite Z
direction from the entry tilt: both send the +M endpoint outward from
the wafer (entry toward −Z, exit toward +Z).


LINEAR SEGMENTS
---------------
A structure may contain straight (linear) sections as well as curved
ones.  The boundary between a curved segment and a linear segment, or
between two linear segments, is treated as the end of an open curve:
the joining face is a flat circle (Blade° = 0) and no Rot° is required.
Within a linear segment all wafers are straight prisms (Blade° = 0 on
both faces, Rot° = 0).


CONSTRUCTING A SINGLE WAFER
----------------------------
Wafer i is defined by row i−1 (entry) and row i (exit).

Given:
    θ_entry = Blade° from row i−1
    θ_exit  = Blade° from row i
    L       = Length from row i
    Rot     = Rot° from row i
    R       = cylinder radius (from the cut list header)

Step 1 — Local frame
    Set the cylinder axis to Z, with Z = 0 at the entry face center and
    Z = L at the exit face center.  Choose an arbitrary initial major-axis
    direction M in the XY plane.

Step 2 — Entry ellipse (at Z = 0)
    The cutting plane dips toward the entry side (−Z) as you move in the
    +M direction (z = −x·tan(θ_entry) in the M–Z cross-section).
    Outward normal pointing toward the entry side (approximately −Z):

        n_entry = −cos(θ_entry)·Z − sin(θ_entry)·M

    θ specifies the magnitude of the tilt; M specifies its direction in
    the XY plane.  Both are required to fully define the plane orientation.

    Minor semi-axis = R  (in direction Z × M, normalised)
    Major semi-axis = R / cos(θ_entry)  (in direction M)
    Mark: the +M endpoint of the major axis (at lower Z, toward entry).

Step 3 — Exit ellipse (at Z = L)
    Exit major-axis direction:  M' = M rotated by Rot° around Z.
    The cutting plane rises toward the exit side (+Z) as you move in
    the +M' direction (z = L + x·tan(θ_exit) in the M'–Z cross-section).
    Outward normal pointing toward the exit side (approximately +Z):

        n_exit = cos(θ_exit)·Z − sin(θ_exit)·M'

    Minor semi-axis = R  (in direction Z × M', normalised)
    Major semi-axis = R / cos(θ_exit)  (in direction M')
    Mark: the +M' endpoint of the major axis (at higher Z, toward exit).

Step 4 — Solid
    The wafer is a portion of a STRAIGHT cylinder (axis along Z, radius
    R) bounded by the two oblique cutting planes.  Construct it by
    creating a cylinder long enough to extend beyond both cutting planes
    and intersecting it with the two half-spaces defined by n_entry and
    n_exit.  Do NOT use a loft between the two ellipses: a loft produces
    a curved or tapered solid, not the correct straight-walled cylinder
    section.


FIRST AND LAST WAFERS
---------------------
Open curve — first wafer (wafer 1):
    Entry face:  flat circle, radius R, no tilt (θ = 0).
                 No mark is needed; the entry circle imposes no rotational
                 constraint.
    Exit face:   constructed from row 1 as described above.
    M:           arbitrary (the starting major-axis direction is a free
                 parameter; it determines the global orientation of the
                 entire assembled structure).

Open curve — last wafer (wafer N):
    Entry face:  constructed from row N−1 as described above.
    Exit face:   flat circle, radius R, no tilt (θ = 0).

Closed curve:
    All wafers are constructed identically using the wraparound:
        Wafer 1  — entry from row N, exit from row 1.
        Wafer i  — entry from row i−1, exit from row i.
        Wafer N  — entry from row N−1, exit from row N.
    There is no implicit row 0 and no special first or last case.


ASSEMBLY
--------
The mating faces of adjacent wafers (exit of wafer i and entry of wafer
i+1) are always congruent ellipses: they share the same blade angle θ_i,
the same cylinder radius R, and therefore the same dimensions.

To join wafer i+1 to wafer i:

    1. Face alignment
       Orient wafer i+1 so that its entry face points toward the exit
       face of wafer i (flip if necessary).

    2. Center alignment
       Translate wafer i+1 until the center of its entry ellipse
       coincides with the center of the exit ellipse of wafer i.

    3. Mark alignment
       Rotate wafer i+1 about the shared face normal (perpendicular to
       the mating ellipses) until the mark on its entry ellipse coincides
       with the mark on the exit ellipse of wafer i.

Steps 2 and 3 together uniquely determine the position and orientation of
each wafer relative to its neighbour.  The marks encode the major-axis
direction of cut i on both mating faces; aligning them ensures that the
accumulated Rot° rotations reproduce the intended 3D shape.


NOTES
-----
Cylinder° column:
    Not used for reconstruction.  It is a physical sawing aid that
    encodes the absolute workpiece orientation on the saw table, including
    a 180° flip convention for alternating wafers.  The mark-alignment
    assembly rule above implicitly handles what Cylinder° addresses during
    sawing.

Initial offset (cut list header):
    Not used for reconstruction.  It is part of the Cylinder° sawing
    guidance system.
